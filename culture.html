Culture

Japanese culuture

Japanese culture is a set of values that puts importance on social harmony and hard work. until the 10th century, Japanese culture was similar to the Chinese culture, but the isolation during the Edo Period changed the rules of society. the culture was influenced by the local Shinto religion, Buddhism, Confucianism and the limited natural resources.Althoug Japanese culture has been westernized Japanese people still do everything possible to preserve their cultural heritage by practicing those traditions an example of this is the tea ceremony or wearing kimono for certain festivals.Inaddtion still following certain traditions or having jobs based on these traditions like being a geisha.

shoes

Taking off shoes when entering someone’s house

masks

Wearing a mask when sick

handshakes

Not shaking hands and not hugging when meeting with loved ones

The yukata

Participating in the firework festivals or bon odori festivals during the summer by wearing a yukata.

food

Making the slurping sound when eating noodles

shrine etiquette

Symbolically washing hands when entering a shrine

holidys

Decorate one's house with dolls for the annual “girls day” and decorate the house with the samurai figure for the annual “boys day”

slippers

Wearing slippers when using the restroom

food for new years

Eating a special meal called "osechi" during the new year's day



Food

Japanses culuture with food

Japanese cuisine is famous for its cultural significance, refined flavors, and delicate presentation. It's one of the most popular and influential cuisines in the world, Japanese cuisine emphasizes fresh, seasonal ingredients that bring out their pure taste and simple texture.
